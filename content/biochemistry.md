# Biochemistry

## DNA and RNA
- DNA base pairing: **Adenine (A)** pairs with **Thymine (T)**; **Guanine (G)** pairs with **Cytosine (C)**.
- In RNA, Thymine is replaced by **Uracil (U)**.
- **Reverse transcriptase** (used by HIV/retroviruses): synthesizes DNA from an RNA template.

## Cellular Energy Metabolism
- **Glycolysis**: breakdown of glucose to pyruvate; occurs in the **cytoplasm (cytosol)**; yields 2 ATP (net) per glucose.
- **Krebs cycle (TCA cycle)**: occurs in the **mitochondrial matrix**; generates NADH and FADH2.
- **Oxidative phosphorylation (electron transport chain)**: occurs on the inner mitochondrial membrane; generates the majority of ATP.
- **Total ATP from complete glucose oxidation**: approximately **30–32 ATP** (net) per glucose molecule.

## Protein Synthesis
- Translation occurs at **ribosomes** (free in the cytoplasm or on rough ER).

## Vitamins
| Vitamin | Deficiency Disease |
|---------|-------------------|
| Vitamin A (retinol) | Night blindness (nyctalopia), xerophthalmia |
| Vitamin C (ascorbic acid) | Scurvy (impaired collagen synthesis — bleeding gums, perifollicular hemorrhage) |
| Vitamin D (calciferol) | Rickets (children), Osteomalacia (adults) |
| Vitamin B1 (thiamine) | Beriberi, Wernicke-Korsakoff syndrome |
| Vitamin B3 (niacin) | Pellagra (diarrhea, dermatitis, dementia) |
| Vitamin B12 (cobalamin) | Megaloblastic (macrocytic) anemia, subacute combined degeneration of spinal cord |
| Vitamin K | Coagulopathy (prolonged PT/INR) |

## Amino Acids
- **Tryptophan**: precursor for **serotonin** (5-HT) and **melatonin**.
- **Tyrosine**: precursor for dopamine, norepinephrine, epinephrine, and thyroid hormones.
- **Phenylalanine**: converted to tyrosine (blocked in phenylketonuria/PKU).

## Clinical Biochemistry
- **HbA1c (glycated hemoglobin)**: reflects the **average blood glucose over the preceding 2–3 months**. Used to monitor long-term glycemic control in diabetes.
