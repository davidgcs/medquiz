# Anatomy

## Skin
The **skin** is the largest organ of the human body by surface area and weight. It serves as a barrier against pathogens, regulates temperature, and provides sensory information.

## Bones
- The **femur** (thigh bone) is the longest and strongest bone in the body.
- The **stapes**, one of the three ossicles in the middle ear, is the smallest bone in the human body.
- Adults have **33 vertebrae** total (cervical: 7, thoracic: 12, lumbar: 5, sacral: 5 fused, coccygeal: 4 fused).

## Heart
The human heart has **4 chambers**: right atrium, right ventricle, left atrium, and left ventricle.
- The **mitral valve (bicuspid valve)** lies between the left atrium and left ventricle.
- The **tricuspid valve** lies between the right atrium and right ventricle.
- **Coronary arteries** supply oxygenated blood directly to the myocardium.

## Cranial Nerves
There are **12 pairs of cranial nerves** (I–XII) arising from the brain and brainstem.

## Spinal Cord
The spinal cord typically ends at the level of **L1–L2** in adults (conus medullaris). Below this is the cauda equina.

## Liver
The liver has **4 lobes**: right, left, caudate, and quadrate. It is the largest gland in the body.

## Kidneys
The kidneys are **retroperitoneal** organs located in the posterior abdominal wall.

## Appendix
The appendix is located in the **right iliac fossa**, arising from the cecum.

## Trachea
The trachea bifurcates into the left and right main bronchi at the **carina**, at the level of **T4–T5** (sternal angle of Louis).

## Circle of Willis
The Circle of Willis is an arterial anastomosis at the base of the **brain** supplying cerebral blood flow.

## Thyroid Gland
The **thyroid gland** is the largest endocrine gland in the body.

## Hyaline Cartilage
Articular surfaces of bones in synovial joints are covered by **hyaline cartilage**, which reduces friction and absorbs shock.

## Phrenic Nerve
The **phrenic nerve** (C3, C4, C5 — "C3, 4, 5 keeps the diaphragm alive") innervates the diaphragm.

## Carpal Tunnel
The carpal tunnel contains the **median nerve** and nine flexor tendons. Compression of the median nerve causes carpal tunnel syndrome.
