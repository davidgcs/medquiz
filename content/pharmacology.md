# Pharmacology

## Anti-inflammatory Drugs
- **Aspirin**: irreversibly inhibits **cyclooxygenase (COX-1 and COX-2)**, reducing prostaglandin and thromboxane synthesis. Used as analgesic, antipyretic, anti-inflammatory, and antiplatelet.

## Antibiotics
- **Penicillins**: inhibit bacterial **cell wall synthesis** by blocking transpeptidase (penicillin-binding proteins / PBPs). Bactericidal.
- **Macrolides** (e.g., azithromycin, erythromycin): bind the **50S ribosomal subunit**, inhibiting translocation and thus protein synthesis.

## Diabetes
- **Metformin**: first-line drug for **type 2 diabetes mellitus**. Decreases hepatic glucose production (biguanide class); does not cause hypoglycemia.

## Anticoagulants
- **Warfarin**: **vitamin K antagonist** — inhibits hepatic synthesis of clotting factors II, VII, IX, and X.

## Lipid-lowering Agents
- **Statins** (e.g., atorvastatin, simvastatin): inhibit **HMG-CoA reductase**, the rate-limiting enzyme in cholesterol synthesis.

## Antidotes and Reversal Agents
- **Naloxone**: **opioid receptor antagonist** — reverses opioid overdose.
- **Flumazenil**: competitive **benzodiazepine receptor antagonist** — reverses benzodiazepine sedation.
- **N-acetylcysteine (NAC)**: antidote for **acetaminophen (paracetamol) overdose** — replenishes glutathione.

## Autonomic Drugs
- **Atropine**: competitive **muscarinic (M) receptor antagonist**. Increases heart rate, reduces secretions, causes mydriasis.

## Antiepileptics
- **Phenytoin**: sodium channel blocker used for seizures. Side effects include **gingival hyperplasia**, hirsutism, and teratogenicity.

## Antihypertensives in CKD
- **ACE inhibitors** (e.g., lisinopril) or **ARBs** are first-line for hypertension with chronic kidney disease and proteinuria — they reduce intraglomerular pressure.
