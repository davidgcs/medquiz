# Pathology

## Cancer Epidemiology
- Most common cancer in **women** worldwide: **breast cancer**
- Leading cause of cancer-related death in **both sexes**: **lung cancer**
- Most common cancer in **men**: prostate cancer (incidence); lung cancer (mortality)

## Venous Thrombosis — Virchow's Triad
Three factors predisposing to thrombus formation:
1. **Stasis** of blood flow
2. **Endothelial injury**
3. **Hypercoagulability**

## Hematologic Malignancies
- **Hodgkin lymphoma**: characterized by **Reed-Sternberg cells** (large binucleated cells with prominent "owl-eye" nucleoli) on histology.
- **Chronic myeloid leukemia (CML)**: caused by the **Philadelphia chromosome** — t(9;22) translocation producing the BCR-ABL fusion oncogene.

## Histopathological Features
- **Psammoma bodies** (laminated calcifications): found in **papillary thyroid carcinoma**, meningioma, serous papillary ovarian carcinoma.
- **Caseous necrosis**: crumbly, cheese-like necrosis characteristic of **tuberculosis** (and other mycobacterial and fungal infections).
- **Cowdry A intranuclear inclusion bodies**: characteristic of **herpes simplex virus (HSV)** and CMV infection.

## Myocardial Infarction (MI)
The most common site of MI is the **left ventricular anterior wall**, supplied by the left anterior descending (LAD) coronary artery.

## Kidney Stones (Nephrolithiasis)
Most common type: **calcium oxalate stones** (~80% of cases).

## Tumor Markers
- **PSA (prostate-specific antigen)**: elevated in **prostate cancer**
- **AFP (alpha-fetoprotein)**: hepatocellular carcinoma, germ cell tumors
- **CA-125**: ovarian carcinoma
- **CEA**: colorectal, pancreatic, gastric carcinoma

## Autoimmune Disease
- **Systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE)**: characterized by anti-dsDNA antibodies and anti-Smith (anti-Sm) antibodies.

## Gallbladder
- **Cholesterolosis**: cholesterol deposits in the gallbladder wall give a "strawberry gallbladder" appearance on gross pathology.
