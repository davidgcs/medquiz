# Microbiology

## Virology
- **HIV**: a **retrovirus** (positive-sense single-stranded RNA) that uses reverse transcriptase to convert its RNA genome into DNA.
- Identified by the **Ziehl-Neelsen (acid-fast) stain**: Mycobacterium tuberculosis (waxy mycolic acids in the cell wall resist decolorization).

## Parasitology
- **Malaria**: caused by protozoa of the genus **Plasmodium** (P. falciparum, P. vivax, P. ovale, P. malariae), transmitted by female Anopheles mosquitoes.

## Bacteriology
### Tuberculosis
- Causative agent: **Mycobacterium tuberculosis** — acid-fast bacillus.

### Staphylococcus
- Gram-positive cocci arranged in **clusters**.
- **S. aureus**: most virulent; can be methicillin-resistant (MRSA).
- MRSA bacteremia is treated with **vancomycin**.

### Streptococcus
- Gram-positive cocci arranged in **chains**.
- **S. pneumoniae**: most common cause of **community-acquired pneumonia** in adults.

### Neisseria
- Gram-negative **diplococci** (kidney bean–shaped pairs).
- **N. gonorrhoeae**: causative agent of **gonorrhea**.
- **N. meningitidis**: causes meningococcal meningitis.

### Cholera
- **Vibrio cholerae**: gram-negative comma-shaped rod producing a profuse "rice-water" diarrhea due to the cholera toxin activating adenylyl cyclase.

### Clostridium
- Gram-positive, spore-forming, obligate anaerobes.
- **C. perfringens**: causes **gas gangrene** (clostridial myonecrosis).
- **C. difficile**: causes antibiotic-associated pseudomembranous colitis.

## Mycology (Fungi)
- **Pneumocystis jirovecii**: causes **Pneumocystis pneumonia (PCP)** in immunocompromised patients (e.g., HIV/AIDS with CD4 < 200 cells/μL).

## Nosocomial Infections
The most common hospital-acquired (nosocomial) infection is **urinary tract infection (UTI)**, accounting for ~30–40% of all healthcare-associated infections.
