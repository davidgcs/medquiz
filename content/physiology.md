# Physiology

## Cardiovascular Physiology
- Normal resting heart rate (adults): **60–100 beats per minute**
- Normal systolic blood pressure: **< 120 mmHg**
- Normal diastolic blood pressure: **< 80 mmHg**
- Normal resting cardiac output: **~5 L/min** (CO = HR × stroke volume)
- Total blood volume in a 70 kg adult: **~5 liters**

### Heart Sounds
- **S1** (lub): closure of the **mitral and tricuspid (AV) valves** — marks the beginning of systole
- **S2** (dub): closure of the **aortic and pulmonary (semilunar) valves** — marks the beginning of diastole

### Cardiac Conduction
The dominant pacemaker of the heart is the **sinoatrial (SA) node** (60–100 bpm intrinsic rate).
Secondary pacemaker: **AV node** (40–60 bpm).

## Body Temperature
Normal core body temperature: **37°C (98.6°F)**

## Blood
- Normal arterial blood pH: **7.35–7.45** (acidosis < 7.35; alkalosis > 7.45)
- Red blood cell (RBC) lifespan: **120 days**
- Platelet lifespan: **7–10 days**
- Erythropoietin (EPO), which stimulates RBC production, is primarily produced by **interstitial cells of the kidney**.

## Renal Physiology
- Normal GFR: **90–125 mL/min**
- **ADH (antidiuretic hormone/vasopressin)** is synthesized in the **hypothalamus** and stored/released from the posterior pituitary.
- The **collecting duct** is the primary site where ADH increases water reabsorption to concentrate urine.

## Respiratory Physiology
- Normal tidal volume (VT): **~500 mL** per breath at rest
- Normal SpO2: **95–100%**
- **Vital capacity (VC)**: maximum air exhaled after maximal inhalation

## Endocrine Physiology
- **Parathyroid hormone (PTH)**: raises blood calcium by increasing intestinal absorption, renal reabsorption, and bone resorption.
